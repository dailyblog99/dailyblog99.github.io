Ask not what you can do for your country. Ask what's for lunch. - Orson Welles

Prague is a great place for someone who is passionate about food. Since its also a tourist hub you will come across almost all popular international cuisine restaurants in the city center. A great way of enjoying a meal at a good price is the Lunch Menu or Polední Menu as its known in Czech. Its basically a soup and a main course. The price for this is generally in the range of 180 - 250 Kč. Here are some suggestions for places you can head to for lunch in Prague

1. Restaurace U Jindřišské věže

Situated right in the city centre this restaurant is a great location for lunches. 10 meters from the Tramstop Jindrisska, this mid sized restaurant packs a punch. This place can get quite busy because of its location. It offers Classic Czech Cuisine and the food is good and priced reasonably. In their lunch menu you can expect the famous goulash, svičkova, smaženy syr, the classics...

2. Ferdinanda Pub, Novomēstka

Not far from U Jindřišské věže on Opletalova you will find this on a crossroad close to Hlavní Nadraži. Its more of a restaurant than a pub. It has two floors for seating. At times they can have their top floor closed for service. They offer Czech Cuisine with a twist. They also brew their own beer which goes by the same name as of the place. I would reccomend the jedenactku along with smazney syr or goulash. The Indian Dal is served as a Čočkova Polevka. Czechs generally have a lentil soup on the first day of a new year.

3.Cafe Imperial

Ok this one is a little expensive one. It has to be. The owner is a celebrity Chef. His name is Zdenek Pohlreich. He has a TV show and he tries to be like Gordon Ramsay. The show can be funny to watch but the food is straight yummy! The lunch menu makes it a bit more affordable.

The classics are of course the goulash, the duck, the rabbit, the buchek ... man typing about these make me hungry.
